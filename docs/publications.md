# Publications

This page summarizes publications and ongoing research related to Reader-Centric Requirements Engineering (RCRE).

## Published Work

### Reader-Centric Requirements Engineering (RCRE): A practical approach for consumable requirements

A previously published paper introduced the foundation of Reader-Centric Requirements Engineering and motivated the need to view requirements not only as authored statements, but also as information artifacts consumed by intended readers.

* **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.infsof.2026.108194
* **Journal:** Information and Software Technology
* **Status:** Published

This publication provides the conceptual basis for the current RCRE research direction. The present work extends this foundation by operationalizing reader-centricity as a construct-based measurement model.

## Manuscript Under Preparation

### Operationalizing Reader-Centricity in Requirements Engineering: A Construct-Based Measurement Model for Consumable Requirements

This manuscript develops a construct-based measurement model for reader-centricity in requirements engineering. It conceptualizes reader-centricity through three dimensions:

* Role-Interrogativity,
* Interpretability,
* Actionability.

The manuscript further explains how Requirements Profiles can instantiate the model for specific reader roles, engineering tasks, required information, and accessible artifacts.

Detailed manuscript information, empirical data, scoring materials, and industrial examples will be updated after submission and review.

## Research Lineage

The current RCRE research program can be understood as a staged development:

```text
Published RCRE foundation
        ↓
Construct-based reader-centricity model
        ↓
Requirements Profile-based operationalization
        ↓
Evidence-based diagnostic assessment
        ↓
Future templates, examples, and tool support
```

## Related Research Direction

RCRE is part of a broader research direction on:

* requirement consumability,
* reader-centric requirements engineering,
* role-specific requirement interpretation,
* evidence-based requirement assessment,
* measurement models in requirements engineering,
* Requirements Profile-based operationalization,
* diagnostic support for downstream requirement use.
